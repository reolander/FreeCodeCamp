A Revolutionary Democrat

Bernie Sanders, resisting arrest during a protest against segregation, in Chicago's public schools.

Here's a time line of Sen. Bernie’s life:

  • 1941 - Born in Brooklyn, New York.
  • 1963 - Took part in the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom, where Martin Luther King, Jr. gave his "I Have a Dream" speech.
  • 1964 - Graduated from the University of Chicago.
  • 1971 - Began his political career as a member of the Liberty Union Party.[31] He was the Liberty Union candidate for Governor of Vermont in 1972 and 1976.
  • 1981 - Was elected mayor of Burlington, Vermont and was re-elected three times.
  • 1991 - Became a United States representative for Vermont's at-large congressional district and was congressman for 16 years.
  • 2006 - Was elected to the U.S. Senate after he won 64.5% of the vote.
  • 2012 - Was re-elected by winning almost 71% of the vote.
  • 2015 - Became a member of the Senate Budget Committee and a candidate for the Democratic Party's nomination for President in the 2016 United States presidential election.
  • 2016 – States “Anybody here who thinks real change comes easy,” Sanders proclaimed, “knows nothing about American history” while talking about political revolution in US.
